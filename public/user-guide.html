Getting Started & Login

Everyone

Caveo CRM is accessible from any modern browser. You will receive your login credentials from your manager or the system administrator.

How to log in

1

Open your browser and go to the Caveo CRM URL provided by your admin.

2

Enter your email address and password, then click Sign in.

3

You will land on your personal Dashboard automatically.

Navigation sidebar

The sidebar on the left has all the main sections grouped by category:

SELL

  • Dashboard — your daily overview
  • Leads — manage lead pipeline
  • Opportunities — deal tracking
  • Pipeline Tasks — your to-do list
  • Analytics — pipeline charts

OPERATE

  • Collections — billing & payments
  • Daily Updates — activity log
  • KRAs — your goals & commits
Tip: Your name and role appear at the bottom of the sidebar. Click Sign out there when you are done for the day.

Lead Pipeline

Everyone

Every sales opportunity begins as a Lead. The Lead Pipeline tracks each prospect from first contact all the way through to Proposal Sent, at which point it becomes an Opportunity.

Lead stages — in order

New Lead → Contacted → Qualified → Req. Gathered → Solution Proposed → POC / Demo → Proposal Sent

Adding a new lead

1

Go to Leads from the sidebar under SELL.

2

Click + New Lead (top right).

3

Fill in the required fields: Lead Title (e.g. "NGFW Replacement Project"), Company Name, Contact Person.

4

Add email, phone, source (Direct / Referral / LinkedIn etc.), and an expected value in ₹L if known.

5

Click Create Lead. A first-call follow-up task is automatically created for you.

Moving a lead through stages

You can use two views — switch between them with the Table / Kanban toggle:

📋 Table View

  • Click a lead row to open its detail page
  • Use the Stage dropdown on the detail page to advance the stage
  • Use the search bar to find leads quickly

🗂 Kanban View

  • Drag a card from one column to the next to update its stage
  • Good for reviewing your entire pipeline at a glance
  • Each column shows the count and total value

Lead detail page

Click any lead to open its full detail page. Here you can:

  • Edit all lead fields and save
  • Change the stage using the dropdown
  • Add Notes (meeting notes, call summaries, etc.)
  • View the Activity Feed — every change is logged automatically
  • Create Tasks linked to the lead
  • Convert to an Opportunity when Proposal Sent stage is reached
Best practice: Always add a brief note after a customer call. Notes are visible to your manager and help maintain continuity if a colleague needs to cover for you.

Opportunities

Everyone

An Opportunity is created once a lead reaches the Proposal Sent stage. It tracks the deal value, probability, and negotiation progress until closure.

Opportunity stages

Proposal Sent → Follow-up → Negotiation → Won ✓ / Lost ✗ / On Hold

Creating an opportunity from a lead

1

Open the Lead detail page (click any lead in the pipeline).

2

Change the stage to Proposal Sent using the stage dropdown.

3

A button Convert to Opportunity appears — click it.

4

Enter the Deal Value (₹L), expected closure date, and probability %.

5

Click Save. The opportunity now appears in the Opportunities page.

Marking a deal as Won

1

Go to Opportunities in the sidebar.

2

Find the deal and open its detail page.

3

Change the stage to Won. The deal value is added to your Won total on the dashboard.

Note: Marking a deal Lost or Won is permanent in terms of pipeline reporting. Make sure the value and close date are accurate before updating.

Pipeline Tasks

Everyone

Tasks keep you on track for follow-ups, demos, proposal submissions, and any other action tied to a lead or opportunity.

Creating a task

1

Go to Pipeline Tasks in the sidebar, or open a lead / opportunity detail page.

2

3

Fill in: Title, Due Date, Priority (Low / Medium / High), and optionally link to a Lead or Opportunity.

4

Click Create. The task appears on your Dashboard and in the Tasks page.

Collections & Billing

Everyone

The Collections page tracks every invoice raised and the payment received against it. Use it to record payment updates so the manager can see outstanding balances in real time.

Adding a new invoice record

1

Go to Collections in the sidebar under OPERATE.

2

Click + Add Invoice.

3

Fill in: Customer Name ✱, Invoice No, Invoice Date, Invoice Value (₹L) ✱, Due Date ✱.

4

The Total (Without GST) auto-fills at 18% GST — click Auto (18%) or enter manually.

5

If payment has already been received, enter Amount Received (₹L) and the Payment Received Date.

6

Set Status (Pending / Partially Received / Fully Received) and click Add.

Updating when payment arrives

1

Find the invoice in the table (use the search bar or filter by Upcoming).

2

Click Edit on the row.

3

Update Amount Received, set Payment Received Date to the actual date payment hit the bank.

4

Change status to Fully Received (or Partially Received for part-payment) and click Update.

Filter tabs

TabShows
AllEvery invoice regardless of status
OverdueInvoices past due date and not fully received (red)
Upcoming (30d)Invoices due within the next 30 days
ReceivedFully paid invoices
Revenue SummaryAggregated billing and collection totals by salesperson
Tip: The Paid On column shows whether payment was on time (green) or late (red with days late). Keeping this updated helps management track payment behaviour by customer.

Daily Updates

Everyone

The Daily Updates log is a lightweight end-of-day summary. Entries are visible to your manager in real time. Think of it as a brief email to your manager — without actually sending one.

Adding a daily update

1

Go to Daily Updates in the sidebar.

2

Click + Add Update.

3

Select the Date (defaults to today), enter a brief summary of what you did, any blockers, and next steps.

4

Click Save.

Recommended: Log updates at the end of each day before signing out. Keep entries concise — 2 to 5 bullet points is ideal. Focus on outcomes, not activities (e.g. "Qualified NGFW lead at ABC Corp — demo booked for Friday" is better than "Sent emails and made calls").

KRAs & Weekly Commits

Everyone

KRAs (Key Result Areas) are your goals assigned by your manager. Each week you make a Weekly Commit — a specific, measurable promise of what you will achieve that week against each KRA.

Understanding your KRAs

Go to KRAs in the sidebar. You will see each active KRA with:

  • Title — what the goal is about (e.g. Pipeline Value, Certifications)
  • Weight — how much this KRA contributes to your overall score (%)
  • Latest Progress — the score your manager last entered
  • Certifications — any certifications you have logged under this KRA

Making a weekly commit

1

On the KRAs page, find the KRA you want to commit on.

2

Click + Add Commit (or the weekly commit area next to the KRA).

3

Write a specific commit — e.g. "Close 2 qualified demos this week" or "Complete Fortinet NSE4 module 3".

4

Click Save. Your commit appears on your Dashboard for the week.

Adding a certification

1

On the KRAs page, find the relevant KRA and click + Cert or Add Certification.

2

Enter: Certification Name, Issuing Body, Date Obtained.

3

Click Submit. The certification goes to your manager for approval (status shows Pending until approved).

Best practice: Make your weekly commit on Monday morning. This sets your own intention for the week and gives your manager visibility without a separate check-in call.

Importing Data (CSV / Excel)

Employees

The Import page lets you upload CSV or Excel files to bulk-create or update records. It's faster than entering rows one by one.

What you can import

📈 Sales / Bookings

  • Required: Customer Name, Opportunity Name
  • Optional: Deal Value, Stage, Territory, Category, GP%, Close Date, Salesperson, Remarks
  • Matched by Customer + Opportunity name — re-uploading updates existing records safely

💰 Collections

  • Required: Customer Name, Invoice Value, Due Date
  • Optional: Invoice No, Invoice Date, Without GST, Amount Received, Payment Received Date, Status, Salesperson, Remarks
  • Matched by Invoice No — safe to re-upload for updates

How to import

1

Go to Import in the sidebar.

2

Select the tab — Sales / Bookings or Collections.

3

Drag & drop your .csv or .xlsx file into the upload area, or click to browse.

4

The system auto-detects column names. Review the Map Columns panel — each column in your file maps to a CRM field. Adjust if any column was not detected correctly.

5

If your file does not have a Salesperson column, select a Default Salesperson from the dropdown — all rows will be assigned to them.

6

Check the Preview (first 5 rows) to confirm the mapping looks correct.

7

Click Import / Update N rows. You will see a summary of how many rows were inserted, updated, or skipped (with reasons).

Column name tips

The importer is flexible — it recognises many variations automatically. For example, the Payment Received Date field is detected from column names like:

Paid On  ·  Paid Date  ·  Payment Date  ·  Date Received  ·  Date Paid  ·  Settlement Date

Important: The importer processes up to 200 rows per upload. For larger files, split them into batches. Re-uploading the same file is safe — it will update existing records rather than create duplicates.

Need help?

Contact your manager or the system administrator. Common issues:

  • "I can't see my lead" — Check the view filter at the top is not set to a specific stage.
  • "My import skipped rows" — Click Show details on the import result to see the reason for each skipped row.
  • "My task doesn't disappear after marking Done" — Refresh the page (F5). The update was saved, the UI just needs to reload.
  • "I can't log in" — Contact your manager to reset your password.
